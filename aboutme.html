About me

  • Vicissitudes and a spirit of discovery have taken me here and there. Sometimes voyager into the unknown, trying to make sense of the complexity of life, other times into more formal roles
  • My scientific background is in:
    • plant-soil interactions (plant-soil feedbacks, biogeochemestry and nutrient cycling, and plant coexistance)
    • resilience of soil processes to global change drivers and their restoration
    • soil ecosystem services and their spatio-temporal distribution
    • soil threats and their spatio-temporal distribution
  • More recently, I became interested in focusing on understanding whether the dynamics of ecological processes in urban systems follow similar patterns to those seen in other systems such as grasslands, and why or why not?
  • More recently too, I became interested in what makes one's teaching practices in sustainability and the environment relevant for students, technically robust, beyond the today's cliches
  • I am something of an obssessed with knowledge, I guess the negative term for this is dilatantee. Yet I am wise enough to know I will never know everything; so that I concentrate on stuff such as epistemology, science education, history of scientific ideas, among other things.
